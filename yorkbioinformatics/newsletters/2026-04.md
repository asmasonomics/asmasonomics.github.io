  - title: York BTF Data Science Lab
    description: |
      If you want to do some bioinformatics, but don't know where to begin - this is the place! Alastair Droop's team within the TF supports the diverse research, training and technical requirements of the department on a chargeable basis. This ranges from routine data management to bespoke high-level analysis and paper-ready figures. <a href="https://mail.google.com/mail/?view=cm&to=btf-ds-group@york.ac.uk&su=Data%20Science%20Lab%20Enquiry" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Get in touch to discuss your project and requirements today!</a>
      
      The Data Science Lab also runs training courses. Upcoming courses are listed below, or <a href="https://mail.google.com/mail/?view=cm&to=btf-ds-group@york.ac.uk&su=Data%20Science%20Lab%20Training%20Enquiry" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">contact the team</a> to book 1:1 training or register your interest in future courses.
      
    items:
      - title: Introduction to Scientific Computing
        link: https://www.york.ac.uk/biology/technology-facility/data-science/courses/
        image: /yorkbioinformatics/assets/images/intro-to-sci-comp-hero-image.png
        description: >
          On this course, you'll become familiar with the core concepts of Scientific Computing, a skill necessary for handling the sorts of big data generated in your scientific research.
          You'll learn the basics of using Bash to navigate and control your computer from the terminal, through to more advanced techniques like accessing other computers (e.g. High Performance Computing / HPC clusters) remotely.
          We'll also teach you how to use version control systems like Git and remote code repositories like GitHub to manage the code that you write.
        date: 20–21 May 2026
        location: Biology Department, The University of York
        
      - title: Introduction to Python Programming
        link: https://www.york.ac.uk/biology/technology-facility/data-science/courses/
        image: /yorkbioinformatics/assets/images/intro-to-python-course-hero-image.png
        description: >
          Learn the basics of the world's most popular programming language — Python — and then apply your newly-learned skills to begin to analyse scientific data. Suitable for beginners, or those who have dabbled a little in another language.
          We'll cover the fundamentals of programming, then move on to more advanced concepts like functions and classes, before moving on to reading in, processing, and plotting real data.
          By the end, you'll be proficient enough in Python to apply it to your everyday work and research.
        date: 27–28 May 2026
        location: Biology Department, The University of York

    description: |

These are great sessions to present developing work and are all about building a regional bioinformatics network. The focus is being a "user group" rather than a polished conference. <br/>
